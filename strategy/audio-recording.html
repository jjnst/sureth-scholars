Audio Recording Script

Script for recording a native Sureth speaker. The recording will be used for AI voice cloning (ElevenLabs) and direct pronunciation clips on the site.

~15Minutes Total
6Sections
40+Words Covered
WAVPreferred Format

Part 1 — Letter Names & Sounds

~3 minutes

Say the letter name, pause briefly, then say the sound.

Alap … A Beth … B Gamal … G Dalat … D Heh … H Waw … W Zayn … Z Khet … KH (deep throat) Teth … T (emphatic) Yodh … Y Kap … K Lamadh … L Meem … M Noon … N Simkath … S Ayn … (guttural vowel) Peh … P Sadeh … TS (emphatic) Qop … Q (deep guttural) Resh … R Sheen … SH Taw … T

Part 2 — Soft Variants (BGDKPT)

~1 minute

Say the letter name, then the soft sound.

Beth … V / W (soft) Gamal … GH (soft) Dalat … TH (soft, as in "that") Kap … KH (soft) Peh … F (soft) Taw … TH (soft)

Part 3 — Vowels / Zow'e

~2 minutes

Say the vowel name, the sound, then the English comparison.

Zqapa … AA … as in "father" Pthakha … UH … as in "verbal" Zlama Psheeqa … IH … as in "sit" Zlama Qashya … EH … as in "bed" Rwakha … OH … as in "phone" Rwasa … OO … as in "moon" Khwasa … EE … as in "bee"

Part 4 — Mnemonic Drill with Beth

~1 minute

Read in a steady rhythm.

Beth Zqapa BAA Beth Pthakha BUH Beth Zlama Psheeqa BI Beth Zlama Qashya BEH Beth Rwakha BOH Beth Rwasa BOO Beth Khwasa BEE

Part 5 — Common Words

~3 minutes

Say each word clearly, twice. Pause between repetitions.

Family Aaba (father)  ·  Yimma (mother)  ·  Broona (son)  ·  Bratha (daughter) Akha (brother)  ·  Khatha (sister)  ·  Baba (grandfather)  ·  Nanna (grandmother)
Greetings & Phrases Shlama (peace / hello) Shlama alookhon (hello to you all) Tawdi (thank you) Baseema (good / pleasant) Dakhee wet? (how are you? — to a male) Dakhee wat? (how are you? — to a female) Spay (fine / well) Belaya (yes)  ·  La (no) Khosh gelokh (welcome — to a male) Khosh gelakh (welcome — to a female)
Everyday Words Beta (house)  ·  Maaya (water)  ·  Lakhma (bread) Ktaawa (book)  ·  Yalpa (learning)  ·  Malpana (teacher) Lishana (language)  ·  Yoma (day)  ·  Lilya (night) Shimsha (sun)  ·  Sahra (moon)
Numbers 1–10 Kha (1)  ·  Tre (2)  ·  Tlatha (3)  ·  Arba (4)  ·  Khamsha (5) Ishta (6)  ·  Showa (7)  ·  Tmania (8)  ·  Tsha (9)  ·  Isra (10)
Colors Smoqa (red)  ·  Yaroqa (green)  ·  Zarga (blue) Khwara (white)  ·  Koma (black)  ·  Shaarooqa (yellow)

Part 6 — Natural Speech

~2 minutes

Read these sentences naturally, as if speaking to a friend.

Shlama, shimi Josh. Ana min Bet Nahrain. (Hello, my name is Josh. I am from Mesopotamia.)   Dakhee wet? Ana spay, tawdi. (How are you? I am fine, thank you.)   Bkhzaya lishana d Sureth khasya baseema. (Learning the Sureth language is very good.)   Aha ktaawa raba baseema. (This book is very good.)   Ana baya yalpinna Sureth. (I want to learn Sureth.)

Then speak freely for ~30 seconds about anything — a memory, what you ate today, anything in Sureth. This helps the AI capture natural rhythm and intonation.

Recording Tips

  • Pace: Natural, not too slow. Imagine explaining to a child.
  • Pauses: Brief pause between each item. Longer pause between sections.
  • Consistency: Keep the same distance from the mic throughout.
  • Environment: Quiet room, no echo or background noise.
  • Format: Save as WAV or M4A. MP3 is okay too.
  • File: One continuous recording is fine. We can split it later.
